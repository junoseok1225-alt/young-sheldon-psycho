Who is Young Sheldon?

Young Sheldon Cooper, a child prodigy from the show Young Sheldon, displays fascinating behaviour shaped by his environment, upbringing, and personality. This project analyses his behaviour using five key psychological approaches.

Behaviourist Approach

The behaviourist approach focuses on observable behaviour and learning through rewards and punishments. In Young Sheldon, Sheldon's reactions to praise or correction at school and home demonstrate this approach in action.

Social Learning Approach

This approach emphasises learning through observation and imitation. Sheldon often models behaviour from his family and teachers, showing how social learning influences his development.

Biological Approach

The biological approach looks at genetics, brain structures, and neurochemistry. Sheldon's high intelligence and memory can partly be explained by innate biological factors.

Psychodynamic Approach

This approach examines unconscious drives and early experiences. Sheldon's interactions with his family and his need for routines reveal underlying unconscious motivations and conflicts.

Cognitive Approach

The cognitive approach focuses on mental processes such as thinking, memory, and problem-solving. In Young Sheldon, we can see how Sheldon's incredible memory, logical thinking, and attention to detail influence his behaviour and interactions.
