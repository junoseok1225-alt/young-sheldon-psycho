Young Sheldon Cooper is a child prodigy whose behaviour can be analysed using different psychological approaches. Click on an approach above to see examples and learn more!

Behaviourist Approach

Sheldon learns through rewards and punishments. For example, when his teacher praises him for correctly solving a math problem, he repeats the behaviour to gain approval.

Social Learning Approach

Sheldon often imitates his older brother or observes his parents’ behaviour. For example, he copies George Sr.’s way of explaining things logically to impress others.

Biological Approach

Sheldon’s high intelligence and memory are partly genetic. His ability to remember long sequences or formulas shows innate biological factors at work.

Psychodynamic Approach

Sheldon’s strict routines and need for order reflect unconscious motivations shaped by childhood experiences and family dynamics.

Cognitive Approach

Sheldon uses logic and reasoning to solve problems. His ability to plan experiments and understand complex theories demonstrates cognitive processing in action.
